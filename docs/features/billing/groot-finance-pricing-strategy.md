## AI Chat Message Limits

### Allocation Model

- **Per-business** (not per-user) - the entire organisation shares a monthly message pool
- **Monthly reset** - unused included messages do not roll over
- **Counter tracks** all messages sent by any team member within the business

### Rationale for Per-Business

- Typically 2-3 people actively use AI chat (owner + finance admin)
- Employees primarily use the app for expense submissions and leave requests
- Per-user limits would be too generous (20 users × 30 = 600 messages for Starter)
- Per-business creates natural upgrade pressure on the decision-makers who actually use chat

### Expected Usage Patterns

**Starter (30 msg/mo, 20 pax):**
- 2-3 active chat users (owner + finance admin)
- ~10 msgs/user/month = hits limit in week 3
- Upgrade trigger: running out of messages by month 2-3

**Pro (300 msg/mo, 50 pax):**
- 5-8 active chat users (owner + finance team + managers)
- ~40-60 msgs/user/month = sustainable for daily use
- Comfortable for a 50-person company

**Enterprise (Unlimited):**
- Any number of active users
- No restrictions

## Credit Packs (Add-On)

For businesses that need additional AI chat messages beyond their plan allocation.

| Pack | Messages | Price | Expiry | Cost/Message |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Boost** | +50 messages | RM 79 | 90 days from purchase | RM 1.58 |
| **Power** | +150 messages | RM 199 | 90 days from purchase | RM 1.33 |

### Credit Pack Design Principles

1. **Pressure valve, not a solution** - credits prevent frustration but are intentionally less economical than upgrading
2. **90-day expiry** - fairer than monthly expiry for purchased packs, but prevents indefinite stockpiling
3. **Priced to encourage Pro upgrade** - a Starter user buying 2 Boost packs/month spends RM 407 for 130 msgs; Pro gives 300 msgs + full AR/AP for RM 599

### Expected Upgrade Funnel

```
Month 1: Starter user has 30 msgs. Uses ~25. "This is fine."
Month 2: Uses all 30. Runs out day 22. Buys 1 Boost (RM 79). Total: RM 328 for 80 msgs.
Month 3: Runs out again. Buys 2nd Boost. Now paying RM 407/mo for 130 msgs.
Month 4: Realises RM 407 for 130 msgs vs RM 599 for 300 msgs + full AR/AP.
Month 5: Upgrades to Pro.
```

## SST on FinanSEAL Subscriptions

### Malaysia

FinanSEAL's SaaS subscription is a **taxable service** under Malaysia's Service Tax Act.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Service tax rate | **8%** (effective since 2024, raised from 6%) |
| Pricing display | **Exclusive of SST** (prices shown + 8% SST) |
| Starter effective price | RM 249 + RM 19.92 SST = **RM 268.92** |
| Pro effective price | RM 599 + RM 47.92 SST = **RM 646.92** |
| Invoice display | Line item: "Service Tax (8%): RM XX.XX" |
| Registration threshold | RM 500,000 annual taxable service revenue |

**Decision: Prices shown EXCLUSIVE of SST.** This is standard B2B SaaS practice in Malaysia. Stripe handles tax calculation and collection.

## Singapore E-Invoice Strategy (InvoiceNow / Peppol)

### Background

Singapore uses **InvoiceNow** (based on the Peppol network) for e-invoicing. IMDA is Singapore's Peppol Authority.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Network | Peppol (Pan-European Public Procurement Online) |
| SG brand | InvoiceNow |
| Authority | IMDA (Info-communications Media Development Authority) |
| Identifier | UEN (Unique Entity Number) |
| Status | IRAS requires GST-registered businesses to transmit invoice data via InvoiceNow |
| Rollout | Progressive from Nov 2025 (revenue > SGD 10M first), expanding through 2026 |

### FinanSEAL Strategy

Since both Malaysia (LHDN MyInvois) and Singapore (InvoiceNow) use **Peppol-based protocols**, a single partnership can cover both markets.

| Market | Protocol | FinanSEAL Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Malaysia | LHDN MyInvois (Peppol) | Starter: 100/mo, Pro: Unlimited |
| Singapore | InvoiceNow (Peppol) | Starter: 100/mo, Pro: Unlimited |

**Action:** When selecting e-Invoice API partner, ensure they are Peppol-certified for **both** MY and SG markets. This avoids separate integrations.

## Payroll Strategy

### Current State

FinanSEAL does **NOT** include payroll. This is a known gap vs Financio (RM 75/mo) and Swingvy (included).

### Strategic Decision: Partner, Not Build

| Option | Effort | Timeline | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build payroll | 6-12 months, complex (EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB for MY; CPF, SDL for SG) | Year 2+ | **No** - too complex, diverts from AI focus |
| Partner (referral) | 1-2 weeks | Immediate | **Yes** - refer to Swingvy/Talenox |
| Partner (integration) | 2-3 months | Post-launch | **Yes** - CSV export templates for payroll tools |

### Messaging

- **Do NOT claim** "replaces your entire stack" (we don't replace payroll)
- **DO claim** "replaces expense management, leave management, invoicing, and accounting — and integrates with your payroll provider"
- Provide **CSV export templates** for Swingvy, PayrollPanda, Kakitangan, SQL Payroll (already in Pro tier as `feature_export_templates`)

### Impact on Stack-Replacement Math

With payroll excluded, the honest stack comparison is:

| What FinanSEAL Replaces | Competitor Equivalent | Monthly Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Expense management + OCR | Xero Expenses (RM 16/user × 20) = RM 320 | RM 320 |
| Leave management | Swingvy Leave module (~RM 4/user × 20) = RM 80 | RM 80 |
| Basic invoicing + accounting | Bukku Grow or Financio Essentials | RM 50-65 |
| AI chat assistant | No equivalent | Unique |
| **Total replaced** | | **RM 450-465** |
| **FinanSEAL Starter** | | **RM 249** |

Even excluding payroll, FinanSEAL Starter at RM 249 is **46% cheaper** than the tools it directly replaces. Customers keep their existing payroll provider.

- FinanSEAL adds: full AI suite (300 msgs, anomaly detection, action center), vendor price intelligence, batch submissions, duplicate detection, advanced leave, audit trail
